# German Plan: A1 to B2 in 6 Months

Date: 2026-03-31

## Reality Check

Going from A1 to B2 in 6 months is possible, but only if you treat it like a serious project.

This is not a casual pace.

To reach B2 in 6 months from A1, you should expect:

- 3 to 4 hours per day on weekdays
- 4 to 6 hours per day on weekends
- daily speaking
- daily listening
- daily vocabulary review
- weekly writing correction

If you average less than that, B1 is more realistic than B2.

## Main Goal

By the end of 6 months, you should be able to:

- speak about daily life, study, work, and goals with reasonable fluency
- understand most slow-to-normal everyday German
- write structured emails and short professional texts
- handle job-related basic conversations
- survive German-heavy internship and working-student environments much better

## Priority for Your Situation

Your target is not literary German.

Your target is:

- everyday survival German
- university German
- workplace German
- recruiter and interview German
- SAP / office / process vocabulary

## Important Warning

The biggest reason this plan fails is not grammar difficulty.

It fails when:

- speaking is postponed
- listening is too easy
- vocabulary is not reviewed daily
- writing is never corrected
- people study only textbook German and ignore real spoken German

## Recommendation for You

Because many Germany SAP jobs ask for `sehr gut Deutsch`, your practical target should be:

1. reach strong A2 fast
2. push to reliable B1
3. build job-search and office German early
4. aim for B2, but do not wait for perfect German before applying to internships and student roles

Your German and SAP plan should run in parallel, not one after the other.
