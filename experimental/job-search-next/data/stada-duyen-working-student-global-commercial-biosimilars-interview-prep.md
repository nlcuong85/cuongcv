## 6A. Sample Answers

These are recruiter-safe sample answers Duyen can use directly and then personalize in her own voice.

### 1. Why do you want to join STADA and this Global Commercial Biosimilars role?
What attracts me to STADA is that the company combines commercial ambition with a clear healthcare purpose. I am especially interested in biosimilars because they connect science, access, and real patient impact. This role is a strong fit for me because it combines brand support, launch planning, competitive analysis, metrics, and communication, which are all areas I have supported in healthcare environments. I also like that the role is global and cross-functional, because I enjoy working where analysis and coordination can directly support better decisions and better access to therapy.

### 2. What do biosimilars mean to you, and why are they important?
To me, biosimilars are approved biologic medicines that are highly similar to their reference products and have no clinically meaningful differences in quality, safety, or efficacy. They are important because they can help expand patient access to proven therapies, especially in serious or chronic conditions. I also understand that biosimilars are complex products developed from living systems, so communication around them needs to be accurate, clear, and science-based. That is one reason I find this area so interesting.

### 3. How does your background fit this role?
I believe my background fits this role from both the scientific and commercial sides. I hold a Bachelor of Pharmacy, so I am comfortable with medical terminology and healthcare concepts. At the same time, I am currently pursuing an M.Sc. in Business Management, which has strengthened my understanding of strategy, marketing, and data-based decision-making. In my recent roles at Mead Johnson, AstraZeneca, and Menarini, I supported brand activities, communication initiatives, market analysis, promotional materials, and performance tracking. That combination matches well with the needs of this role.

### 4. Tell us about a time you supported brand planning or launch preparation.
At Mead Johnson Nutrition, I supported brand planning and go-to-market activities across the infant and maternal nutrition portfolio. Part of my role was collecting market and competitor information, supporting portfolio discussions, and helping coordinate materials and campaign-related activities with internal teams and agencies. What I learned from that experience is that strong brand support is not only about ideas, but also about structured execution, timely follow-up, and making sure decisions are supported by clear information. That is a mindset I would bring to this role as well.

### 5. Tell us about a time you gathered and analyzed competitive intelligence or market insights.
At Mead Johnson, I regularly supported market and competitor analysis to identify growth opportunities and support portfolio decisions. I was not just collecting information, but organizing it in a way that could help the team see patterns, market positioning, and areas where communication or commercial focus might need adjustment. I enjoy this kind of work because it sits between analysis and action. Good competitive intelligence should help a team decide what matters most, where to focus, and how to respond more effectively.

### 6. How have you used metrics or data insights to improve decisions or execution?
In my recent roles, I used performance indicators such as campaign engagement and open rates to understand what was working and where adjustments were needed. For example, at Mead Johnson, I monitored campaign performance using CRM and SAP-related tools, and one campaign reached a 42 percent open rate compared with an approximate 30 percent benchmark. For me, metrics are useful because they help move a discussion from assumptions to evidence. I like using data to identify what should be improved next, whether that is timing, messaging, or channel execution.

### 7. Describe your experience creating or revising promotional materials.
I have supported the creation and coordination of promotional and digital materials in regulated healthcare environments, especially at Mead Johnson and Menarini. In these situations, I worked with internal stakeholders and external agencies to make sure materials were aligned with the objective, the brand direction, and the relevant guidelines. What I bring to this type of work is structure and attention to detail. I understand that materials are not only creative outputs, but also business tools that need to be accurate, useful, and appropriate for the audience.

### 8. How have you worked with external agencies or multiple stakeholders?
In several of my roles, I worked closely with both internal teams and external agencies. I learned that the key is to keep communication clear, expectations aligned, and feedback structured. At Mead Johnson and Menarini, for example, I supported coordination around content and promotional activities, which required balancing timelines, input from different stakeholders, and execution quality. I am comfortable following up, clarifying the next step, and helping move work forward in a practical way.

### 9. How would you support communication activities in a global specialty-care setting?
I would support communication activities by focusing on clarity, relevance, and coordination. In specialty care, communication needs to be accurate and aligned with the scientific context, but it also needs to be understandable and useful for the intended audience. In a global setting, I think it is also important to stay organized, communicate clearly across functions, and make sure materials or messages remain consistent while still supporting local execution needs. My background in healthcare communication and regulated environments would help me contribute in that kind of setting.

### 10. Tell us about a time you organized or supported an event, education initiative, or conference.
At AstraZeneca, I coordinated educational programs and communication initiatives for healthcare professionals across multiple institutions. This required planning, communication, and close coordination with different stakeholders to make sure the programs ran smoothly and remained useful for the audience. I enjoyed that work because it combined organization with impact. It also taught me how important clear coordination is when there are several participants, deadlines, and expectations involved.

### 11. How do you work when direction is limited?
When direction is limited, I usually start by clarifying the objective and identifying the most practical next steps. I try to structure the task, define what information is needed, and move forward instead of waiting too long for perfect clarity. At the same time, I check assumptions early and keep stakeholders informed so that I stay aligned. In my experience, being proactive does not mean working alone without communication. It means helping the work progress while staying responsible and organized.

### 12. Why should we hire you if your experience is adjacent rather than directly in biosimilars?
I think my value for this role comes from the combination of scientific understanding, healthcare commercial experience, and strong motivation to work in biosimilars. While I have not worked directly in biosimilars yet, I have already supported brand, communication, and analysis work in regulated healthcare environments, and I have a pharmacy background that helps me understand the scientific side more quickly. I also see biosimilars as a meaningful field because they help improve access to high-quality therapies. So I believe I can contribute from the start while continuing to grow into the specialty.

### 13. What would success look like for you in your first three months?
In the first three months, success for me would mean understanding the team's priorities, the key products and therapy areas, and the way the global team works with other functions and markets. I would also want to become reliable in the core support areas of the role, especially analysis, coordination, materials support, and follow-up. If after three months the team feels they can trust me to contribute in a structured and proactive way, I would consider that a very strong start.

### 14. How would you handle a task where you need to collect information from several internal teams quickly?
I would begin by clarifying exactly what output is needed, by when, and from which teams. Then I would break the request into clear parts, contact the relevant stakeholders with a concise and specific request, and track follow-up closely. I would also summarize the information in a structured way so that the final output is easy to use. If I saw risks around timing or missing input, I would escalate early rather than wait until the deadline. That approach has helped me work effectively in cross-functional environments.

### 15. Why do STADA's biosimilars therapy areas interest you?
What I find especially interesting is that STADA's biosimilars portfolio covers several important therapy areas such as immunology, oncology, ophthalmology, bone health, and nephrology and hematology. That shows that biosimilars are not a narrow category, but an important part of specialty care with real relevance for patients across different conditions. For me, that makes the role even more meaningful, because supporting this work means supporting broader access to important therapies across multiple medical areas.
